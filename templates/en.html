Introduction

A brain stroke, also known as a cerebrovascular accident (CVA), occurs when the blood supply to a part of the brain is disrupted. This can happen due to a blockage in a blood vessel (ischemic stroke) or the rupture of a blood vessel (hemorrhagic stroke). Brain stroke is a medical emergency and requires immediate treatment to prevent serious brain damage or death.

Brain Stroke Risk Factors

There are several risk factors that can increase the likelihood of having a brain stroke. These include:

  • High blood pressure
  • Smoking
  • Diabetes
  • High cholesterol
  • Obesity
  • Sedentary lifestyle
  • Family history of stroke
